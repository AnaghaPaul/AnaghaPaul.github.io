THINGS THAT
GROUND ME

Small, steady influences that help me think clearly — especially when navigating complex problems.

A Quiet Reminder

A small white anthurium plant sits beside my workspace. It grows slowly and steadily, without drawing attention.

It reminds me that progress often comes from consistency, not urgency — a mindset I carry into analytical work.

Music, Structure, and Memory

I am drawn to the work of Rex Vijayan, an experimental film composer from South India known for understated, motif-driven compositions.

His music often reuses melodic ideas across a film, allowing them to evolve with the story rather than repeat mechanically.

Revenge Theme — Parava (2017)

In this piece, a familiar melodic phrase returns in a transformed state. What once felt gentle now carries tension and resolve.

The same musical idea holds two meanings at once — memory of the past and the intensity of the present.

This approach resonates with me because it mirrors how people think: experiences don’t disappear — they resurface, shaped by context.

How This Translates to My Work

Whether I’m analyzing data, debugging a process, or refining a model, I rely on the same principles I admire here: patience, structure, and attention to subtle patterns.

These influences help me stay grounded and focused, especially when working through ambiguity.
