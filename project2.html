Apple Inc. vs S&P 500

A comparative financial analysis exploring risk, return, and diversification using historical stock market data.

Introduction

This project analyzes Apple Inc. alongside the S&P 500 Index to understand how an individual stock performs relative to the broader market. By comparing returns, volatility, and long-term trends, the analysis demonstrates fundamental investment concepts such as risk‚Äìreturn tradeoff and portfolio diversification.

Need of the Study

Investors often face the decision of choosing between individual stocks and market indices. This study helps illustrate how a single high-performing stock like Apple behaves compared to a diversified index such as the S&P 500. Understanding these differences supports informed investment decisions and highlights why diversification plays a critical role in reducing risk.

Dataset

The dataset consists of historical daily stock price data for Apple Inc. (AAPL) and the S&P 500 Index (^GSPC), sourced from Yahoo Finance using the yfinance Python library.

  • 6,427 total records
  • 7 fields including Open, High, Low, Close, Adjusted Close, and Volume
  • Time-series format suitable for return and volatility analysis

Key Findings

  • Apple consistently delivers higher returns than the S&P 500.
  • These higher returns come with significantly higher volatility.
  • The S&P 500 demonstrates more stable behavior due to diversification.
  • Major market events amplify Apple‚Äôs price movements more than the index.

Conclusion

This project highlights the classic risk‚Äìreturn tradeoff in financial markets. While individual stocks like Apple may offer higher growth potential, they also introduce greater uncertainty. Broad market indices provide stability, reinforcing the importance of diversification in long-term investment strategies.
